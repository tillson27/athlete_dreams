# Product Brief — Fund an Athlete's Dream (FAD)

## What it is

The world's most transparent athlete funding network. A directory-style platform where athletes raise money from their network for specific events, gear, travel, and training — with every dollar tied to a line item and every event closing with a post-event update.

## Three pillars

1. **Crowdfunding** *(go-to-market anchor)* — athlete-led campaigns. We onboard the first 20 athletes ourselves and run as their de-facto agent until the directory has flywheel.
2. **Corporate sponsorships** — once the directory has critical mass, brands sign single-event or season-long deals with athletes whose values align.
3. **Managed ambassador programs** — enterprise brands hand us their entire ambassador funnel. We do sourcing, intake, pipeline, and reporting on a quarterly retainer.

## Audiences

- **Athletes** — amateur to pro, not necessarily technical, often first-time fundraisers. They want their story told and their bills paid. They are the primary user.
- **Supporters** — family, friends, fans, alumni associations, neighbourhood groups. They want to give to a specific thing and see where it lands.
- **Brand marketing teams** — running an ambassador program with limited internal headcount. They want a vetted shortlist, not an inbox of unsolicited DMs.

## Differentiators

- **Money moves directly** to the athlete — unlike Makeachamp, which holds funds.
- **Cross-sport directory** with a real story-first profile — unlike Sportfunder.
- **Athletes lead, sponsors layer on** — unlike OpenSponsorship, which lists athletes as inventory.
- **Cost transparency** — every campaign itemizes the spend. Backers fund a line.
- **Post-event updates** — the loop closes with a photo, a result, and a thank-you.
- **Minimalist UX** — athletes and brand marketers are not technical. The platform must read in under 10 seconds.

## Competitive landscape

- **Makeachamp** — money gets held back; athletes complain about cash flow.
- **Sportfunder** — sport-narrow, feature-thin.
- **OpenSponsorship** — brand-first marketplace; athletes are filtered, not featured.
- **GoFundMe** — generic; no athlete narrative, no event-specific structure.

## Pilot plan

- Cohort of 10–20 athletes hand-onboarded over the first 90 days.
- FAD acts as agent — writing copy, sourcing photos, helping shape the cost breakdown.
- Goal: 5–10 successful campaigns and 2–3 brand intros before opening self-serve.

## Pricing

- **Crowdfunding** — 3% platform fee on successful donations. No upfront cost to athletes.
- **Brand sponsorships** — facilitation fee per contract; TBD.
- **Managed ambassador programs** — flat quarterly retainer; TBD per scope.

## Geography

- Canada-first. Founders are based in Canada; first cohort weighted to Canadian athletes. US donations and brand deals are in scope from day one. NIL tooling for US college athletes is on the roadmap but out of scope for the pilot.
